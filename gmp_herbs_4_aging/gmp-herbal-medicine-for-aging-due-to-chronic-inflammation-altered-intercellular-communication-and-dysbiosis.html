Chapter 8 GMP Herbal Medicine for Aging due to Chronic Inflammation, Altered Intercellular Communication, and Dysbiosis

Chronic inflammation is defined by the prolonged presence of pro-inflammatory cytokines and persistent activation of the innate immune system in the absence of active injury or infection. It can arise from various factors, including unresolved pathogen infections, repeated exposure to antigens or toxins, autoimmune disorders, immunosenescence, obesity and physical inactivity, tobacco and alcohol use, chronic physiological or psychological stress, disrupted sleep and circadian rhythms, and gut microbiome dysbiosis.

In traditional Chinese medicine, herbal prescriptions frequently used to address aging-related conditions linked to chronic inflammation include: Dānshēn, Xuèfǔzhúyūtāng, Niúxī, Fùzǐ, Yùjīn, Shūjīnghuóxuètāng, Dàhuáng, Shēngmàiyǐn, Bǔzhōngyìqìtāng, Zhūlíngtāng, Jìshēngshènqìwán, Guīpítāng, Huǒmárén, Héshǒuwū, Dùzhòng, Shānzhā, Tiānmágōuténgyǐn, Gānlùyǐn, Língguìshùgāntāng, Dāngguīniāntòngtāng, and Sānqī. Acupuncture, another modality commonly co-prescribed for chronic inflammation, exerts anti-inflammatory effects through neuro-immune regulatory mechanisms, as reviewed by N. Li et al. (2021).

8.1 Dyskinesia

Dyskinesia is involuntary, excessive movements, such as tremors and tics, of one or multiple muscles of the body including the face, limbs, and trunk. One of the first-line treatments for Parkinson’s disease is levodopa, whose side effects include dyskinesia. Neuroinflammation was reviewed to contribute to levodopa-induced dyskinesia in Parkinsonian patients receiving levodopa therapy (Cardinale et al., 2024). Furthermore, it was found that an antibiotic that decontaminates the intestine improves levodopa-induced dyskinesia in Parkinsonian patients (Baizabal-Carvallo et al., 2021). The global prevalence of first- and second-generation antipsychotic-induced tardive dyskinesia was estimated to be 30% and 21%, respectively (Carbon et al., 2017).

8.2 Headache

The prevalence of headache decreases with increasing age; however, a major neurological complaint in the elderly is headache. Older people often experience tension headache or migraine; the former presents squeezing pain around the head and the latter manifests throbbing pain on one side of the head. Older people also suffer from headaches secondary to an underlying condition such as cervical spine disease, hypertension, and medication overuse. Migraines are believed to be caused by inflammatory responses to the activation and sensitization of the neurons of the trigeminovascular system (Spekker et al., 2021). Tension headaches are caused by contraction of the muscles of the scalp and neck, and are predominantly treated by nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs. A study found an association between depression and medication overuse headache and between obstructive sleep apnea and hypertension-induced headache in patients over 50 years of age (Togha et al., 2022).

8.3 Vertigo

Vertigo is the feeling of moving or spinning of the surrounding when there is actually no such motion. A related term is dizziness, which is the feeling of floating and light-headedness. Vertigo in the elderly often originates from audio-vestibular disorders (Fancello et al., 2023), which develop and progress as a result of age-related inflammation and oxidative stress (H. Chen et al., 2022). The risk of vertigo was also found to be associated with the abundance of specific gut microbiota taxa in humans with the taxa-specific association being mediated through depression or obesity (Q. Rong et al., 2024). The prevalence of vertigo and dizziness in people aged 60 years and older was reported to be around 30% (Fernández et al., 2015).

8.4 Constipation

Constipation, a bowel symptom, occurs when bowel movements become infrequent or stool passing becomes hard. The prevalence of constipation increases with age: 26% of women and 16% of men aged 65 years and older are constipated; 34% of women and 26% of men aged 84 years and older are constipated (De Giorgio et al., 2015). It was shown that high-fructose or high-fat feeds cause changes in intestinal microbiota to induce chronic intestinal inflammation and constipation in mice (R. Tan et al., 2021). Moreover, higher inflammatory potential of the diet was found to increase the risk of constipation in adults (W. F. Lu et al., 2025). Neurological diseases, including Parkinson’s disease and multiple sclerosis, and cardiovascular diseases, including angina and myocardial infarction, were found to be linked to chronic constipation in community residents (Choung et al., 2015).

8.5 Hypertension

Hypertension is when the pressure in blood vessels is too high: 120/80 mmHg or higher. Globally, one third of people aged 30 to 79 years had hypertension (NCD Risk Factor Collaboration, 2021); however, 70% American adults over 65 years of age were hypertensive (Mozaffarian et al., 2015). Recent studies have observed increased systemic inflammation to precede blood pressure elevation; inflammatory infiltration to the renal interstitium and vascular wall culminate the elevated blood pressure (Solak et al., 2016); and anti-inflammatory drugs were suggested to treat hypertension (Dinh et al., 2014). 8.10 Stroke

A stroke occurs when part of the brain does not get enough oxygen and nutrient because a blood vessel leading to or within the brain is blocked or ruptured. Without oxygen, the brain cells in the affected region of the brain will die. Strokes are the leading cause of serious long-term disability. The prevalence of stroke increases with age, reaching ~7% in the elderly (Rajati et al., 2023). The pathobiology and outcomes of a stroke are determined by the local and systemic inflammatory response to the hypoperfused vessels and ischemic brain parenchyma; and gut microbiota and brain ischemia interact with each other through the intestinal immune system (Anrather & Iadecola, 2016). Older stroke patients comorbid with diabetes or depression were found to be associated with difficulty in activity of daily living (Ruksakulpiwat et al., 2023).

8.13 Aneurysm

An aneurysm is an abnormal bulge in a blood vessel because of weakening of the wall of the bulging blood vessel. Abdominal aortic aneurysms are common in men over 65 years old (Sakalihasan et al., 2018) and the chance of brain aneurysm rupture increases with age (UCAS Japan & UCAS II Investigators, 2015). Recruitment and infiltration of inflammatory effectors to aneurysmal walls are prominent feature of aortic aneurysms (Z. Yuan et al., 2021) and cerebral aneurysms (Hudson et al., 2013), demonstrating the critical roles of inflammation in the formation, progression and rupture of aneurysms. Abdominal aortic aneurysms are associated with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, smoking, and coronary artery disease (Müller et al., 2019).

8.14 Hypercholesterolemia

Hypercholesterolemia is the presence of high levels of cholesterol, a type of fat, in the blood. 8.20 Low hemoglobin

Hemoglobin is an iron-containing protein that binds oxygen in red blood cells to facilitate transportation of oxygen from the lungs to body tissues. Hemoglobin concentration declines with age with the declining being more significant in men than in women (Salive et al., 1992). Low levels of red blood cells or hemoglobin due to chronic inflammation is the most common anemia in hospitalized and chronically ill patients (Weiss et al., 2019). The gut microbial composition was found to be less rich in patients with anemia of inflammation; and washed microbiota transplantation restored hemoglobin levels in the patients by increasing the abundance of hemoglobin correlated bacteria genera (H. 8.21 High ferritin

Ferritin, present in the cytosol of every cell, helps keep iron in a safe form for iron storage and release. The level of serum ferritin, which correlates with total iron storage in the body, was found to rise with age (Casale et al., 1981). Glycation due to hyperglycemia can damage ferritin, resulting in leakage of free iron. Excessive free iron generates highly reactive ROS via the Fenton reaction, causing oxidative damage to biomolecules. Ferritin levels were indeed found to be significantly higher in geriatric patients with subclinical inflammatory state (Cankurtaran et al., 2012). Diabetes, arrhythmias, and liver cirrhosis are associated with iron overload (Yassine et al., 2023).

8.22 Low immunoglobulin

Immunoglobulins are antibodies, produced and secreted from B cells into blood and tissue fluids such as saliva, tears, and respiratory and intestinal secretions, to destroy or neutralize specific antigens, i.e., bacteria and viruses, as part of the body’s adaptive immune defense. Immunoglobulin concentration declines with age in healthy adults (Buckley & Dorsey, 1970; Challacombe et al., 1995). Individuals with blood immunoglobulin deficiency commonly manifest fatigue driven by low-grade systemic inflammation (Wågström et al., 2022). Acquired antibody deficiency can be caused by hematologic malignancies such as chronic lymphocytic leukemia, multiple myeloma, and lymphoma, and by protein-losing diseases such as nephrotic syndrome and Crohn’s disease (Patel et al., 2019).
